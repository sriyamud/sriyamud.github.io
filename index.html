UC Berkeley MIDS · Statistical Methods

Key finding: Danceability's positive effect on popularity diminishes significantly for acoustic tracks (interaction term, p < 0.05), suggesting production style moderates rhythmic appeal.

Can AI-Driven Financial Tools Improve Literacy for Underbanked Communities?

UC Berkeley MIDS · Research Design

Designed a mixed-methods experimental study (n=600) with three treatment arms — AI-driven tool, traditional program, and pure control — to evaluate financial literacy interventions for underserved populations.

Developed stratified random sampling, power analysis (Cohen's f=0.25), and a multi-method statistical plan (ANOVA, paired t-tests, Wilcoxon, regression). Addressed algorithmic fairness and self-reported bias risks.

Hypothesis: AI-driven financial tools will produce stronger improvements in literacy, engagement, and retention than traditional programs for underserved communities — a research gap that existing AI-vs-traditional literature hasn't specifically addressed.
Tools: Research Design · Power Analysis · Survey Methodology

COVID and Crime in Los Angeles

UC Berkeley MIDS · Python for Data Science

Analyzed 1M+ LAPD crime records (2020–2025) to investigate how the COVID-19 pandemic reshaped crime patterns across location, time, type, and victim demographics in 21 Los Angeles areas.

Key findings: 25% increase in victimless crimes post-COVID. 8.3% rise in crimes against Asian Americans — consistent with national AAPI hate crime trends. Homicide timing shifted between periods while most other crime types remained stable.
Tools: Python · pandas · matplotlib · NumPy

Predicting Flight Departure Delays at Scale

UC Berkeley MIDS · Machine Learning at Scale

Built a distributed binary classification pipeline on 31.7M+ flight records and 630M+ NOAA weather observations to predict US domestic departure delays ≥15 minutes, two hours before scheduled departure.

Progressed through three modeling phases: GBT and MLP baselines with threshold tuning, then full-scale PyTorch MLP with dropout ablation and feature ablation on 2015–2021 data via Databricks.

Key finding: Rolling airport congestion rate was the highest-signal engineered feature. Threshold tuning on Fβ (β=2) — prioritizing recall to minimize missed delay alerts — drove the largest performance gains over default cutoffs.
Tools: PySpark · PyTorch · scikit-learn · Databricks · Python
